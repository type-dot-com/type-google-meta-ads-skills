# Ad Copy Performance

Use this skill when the work is ad copy and asset performance. The goal is to improve message-market fit without overreacting to small asset samples or disrupting active learning.

## Start Here

1. Read any customized ad-copy instructions if available.
2. Discover connected Google Ads, analytics, CRM, landing page, and document tools.
3. Confirm account, campaign scope, timezone, currency, date range, conversion actions, and active brand or compliance rules.
4. Pull responsive search ads, asset performance labels, combinations when available, ad strength, policy status, and landing page URLs.
5. Compare CTR, conversion rate, CPL or CPA, ROAS, asset labels, impressions, and CRM quality.
6. Identify underperforming, fatigued, redundant, disapproved, or off-message assets.
7. Draft replacement headlines and descriptions with the landing page and keyword theme in mind.
8. Recommend tests rather than overwriting many assets at once.

## Asset Analysis

Review:

- low, good, best, learning, or unrated asset labels
- assets with enough impressions to judge
- duplicate or near-duplicate headlines
- pinned assets that may constrain serving
- weak call to action or weak differentiation
- landing page mismatch
- policy limitations or disapprovals
- brand and compliance constraints

Do not remove every low-labeled asset automatically. Check whether the asset is pinned, strategic, legally required, or under-sampled.

## Creative Fatigue

Flag fatigue when:

- CTR declines materially while impression volume is steady
- conversion rate declines without a tracking or landing page explanation
- frequency or audience saturation is visible in connected sources
- the same message has dominated for multiple review cycles
- competitor messaging has shifted and auction pressure increased

## Drafting Rules

When drafting new copy:

- tie each headline to a keyword theme or search intent
- include at least one benefit-led headline and one action-led headline
- avoid unverifiable claims
- respect character limits when known
- preserve required brand, legal, or product language
- keep descriptions specific enough to test

## Output Format

```text
Ad copy performance: <account>, <date window>.
Findings: <asset and ad group issues>.
Replace: <assets recommended for replacement>.
Drafts: <new headlines/descriptions>.
Tests: <test plan>.
Needs approval: <copy or edit approval>.
```
