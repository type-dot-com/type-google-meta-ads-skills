# Meta Creative Fatigue

Use this skill when the work is ad creative, fatigue, or refresh planning. The goal is to identify which concepts are still working, which are tired, and what creative to produce next.

## Start Here

1. Read any customized Meta creative instructions if available.
2. Discover connected Meta Ads, creative library, analytics, ecommerce, CRM, document, and asset tools.
3. Confirm account, campaign scope, date range, timezone, currency, attribution setting, and primary event.
4. Pull ad-level performance, creative summaries, placement breakdowns, video metrics, and recent edit history when available.
5. Compare last 7 days, previous 7 days, and last 30 days.
6. Group ads by creative concept rather than treating each crop or ratio as a new idea.
7. Diagnose fatigue, format gaps, policy risk, and audience-message mismatch.
8. Draft refresh priorities or creative briefs.
9. Do not launch or replace creative unless explicit authority exists.

For formulas and interpretation rules, read `../meta-ads-command-center/references/meta-math.md`.

## Fatigue Signals

Treat fatigue as likely when multiple signals align:

- frequency is rising
- CPM is rising week over week
- link CTR is falling week over week
- CPA is rising or ROAS is falling
- video hook rate or hold rate is falling
- spend is still flowing to the ad or ad set
- there has not been a tracking, landing page, offer, or seasonality change that better explains the decline

Do not call a new ad fatigued before it has enough impressions and spend to judge.

## Creative Review

Review:

- concept: UGC, founder-led, demo, testimonial, social proof, comparison, problem-solution, offer, or educational
- format: static, video, carousel, collection, Reels, Stories, feed
- aspect ratio coverage: 1:1, 4:5, 9:16, and other placement-specific ratios
- first-frame or hook clarity
- primary text, headline, CTA, and offer consistency
- landing page message match
- policy-sensitive claims or personal-attribute language
- repeated ads across ad sets

## Refresh Recommendations

For each refresh recommendation, include:

- current creative or concept
- evidence of fatigue or underperformance
- audience or placement affected
- new creative angle
- format and aspect ratio
- required proof, offer, or landing page support
- risk or approval need

## Output Format

```text
Meta creative fatigue: <account>, <date window>.
Still working: <ads or concepts>.
Fatigued: <ads or concepts with evidence>.
Format gaps: <missing ratios or placements>.
Refresh brief: <new angles and assets needed>.
Needs approval: <copy, design, or launch decisions>.
Blocked: <missing creative or performance data>.
```

If the user asks for new ad copy, draft reviewable creative concepts and copy variants. Do not imply an ad was launched until the connected tool confirms it.
